Global Food Wastage and Requirements Statistics

1.05 billion metric tons of food are wasted globally each year.
Households account for approximately 631 million metric tons, or 60% of total food waste.
About 13% of food is lost during the supply chain before reaching consumers.
If just a quarter of the food currently lost or wasted were saved, it could feed around 870 million people suffering from hunger globally.
The global population is projected to reach around 9.1 billion by 2050, requiring a 70% increase in agricultural production.
